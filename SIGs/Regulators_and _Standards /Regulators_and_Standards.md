	

# Confidential Computing Consortium Regulatory and Standards Special Interest Group (Regulatory & Standards SIG) Formal Charter


## **Affiliation**

The Regulatory & Standards SIG operates under the governance of, and aligned to, the Governing Board of the Confidential Computing Consortium.

## **Purpose**

The Regulatory and Standards Special Interest Group (Regulatory & Standards SIG) exists to support the informed, responsible, and risk-based adoption of Confidential Computing across governmental and industry regulatory frameworks, supervisory guidance, and standards bodies.

The purpose of the Regulatory and Standards SIG is to inform and influence regulatory and standards outcomes by providing objective, vendor-neutral analysis of Confidential Computing capabilities, limitations, and assurance considerations. This SIG will focus on helping regulators and standards organizations evaluate how protections for data in use and Trusted Execution Environments can be appropriately recognized within existing confidentiality, integrity and attestation objectives—without weakening governance, accountability, or supervisory oversight.

## **Mission**

The mission of the Regulatory & Standards SIG is to translate Confidential Computing assurances into regulatory-relevant concepts, promote consistent interpretation of protections for data in use, and support risk-based integration of Confidential Computing within established regulatory, supervisory, and standards frameworks.

## **Regulators and Standards SIG Scope**

#### **In Scope**

* Governmental 	and Industry regulatory frameworks (e.g. NIST CSF and PCI DSS)  
* International and industry standards (e.g. ISO/IEC 42001\)  
* National standards (e.g. BSI, ANSSI)  
* Audit, 	assurance, and evidence expectations  
* Risk management and control alignment  
* Presenting and articulating taxonomy for the terminology related to terms such as data in use, confidential execution, Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) and attestation

#### **Out of Scope**

* Vendor or product endorsements  
* Marketing or competitive analysis  
* Proprietary standards or certifications

### **Objectives**

To advance its mission, the Regulatory & Standards SIG focuses on practical, outcome-oriented objectives that support regulatory clarity, standards alignment, and supervisory confidence. These objectives are designed to ensure Confidential Computing is evaluated and adopted in a manner consistent with existing data protection, data sovereignty, risk management, governance, and assurance expectations.

* Improve regulator and auditor understanding of Confidential Computing  
* Establish clear, regulator-friendly terminology and framework to describe  Confidential Computing capabilities  
* Map Confidential Computing capabilities to regulatory and standards requirements  
* Support standards evolution through vendor- and architecture-neutral input  
* Support regulators and standards bodies in mapping requirements to appropriate implementation options  
* Clarify assurance, auditability, and residual risk from Confidential Computing implementations  
* Identify gaps in documentation and work with other SIGs, internal and external groups to provide appropriate materials

### **Deliverables**

The Regulatory & Standards SIG produces non-binding, vendor- and architecture-neutral outputs intended to inform regulators, auditors, accreditors, standards bodies, and assurance professionals. These deliverables are designed to improve technical understanding, support consistent interpretation, and facilitate risk-based decision-making without prescribing regulatory outcomes. The artifacts we will deliver will focus on (in conjunction with others as appropriate):

* Position papers and white papers  
* Regulatory and standards mappings  
* Reference terminology  
* Risk and control considerations  
* Consultation responses

### **Membership and Governance**

The Regulatory & Standards SIG is open to participation from regulators, standards contributors, auditors, industry practitioners, academic researchers, and other non-commercial experts with relevant subject matter expertise. Participants in the SIG will take part as individual contributors rather than as representatives of their organizations or commercial interests except as noted in meetings and recorded in minutes. The SIG is led by an elected Chair and Vice-Chair and, where appropriate, one or more Co-Chairs, appointed or selected in accordance with Consortium governance. Decisions and outputs are developed through a consensus-driven process. The SIG does not issue binding regulatory interpretations, supervisory guidance, or authoritative standards determinations.

Where voting is required, the representatives of the Premier Members will vote on a majority basis.

The officers (Chair and Vice-Chair) of the SIG can be nominated by any Member of the Consortium, and will be voted on by the Governing Board.  Elections take place annually, and previous nominees and officers may stand on multiple occasions.

### **Operating Principles**

The Regulatory & Standards SIG operates in accordance with principles of neutrality, transparency, and technical rigor. Its work is grounded in practical applicability to real-world regulated environments and is intended to complement, not replace or conflict with, existing regulatory frameworks, supervisory guidance, and standards. All activities and outputs are designed to maintain credibility with regulators, standards bodies, auditors, and other stakeholders through objective analysis and documented outcomes.

All participants are expected to conduct themselves with professionalism, integrity, and respect, and to adhere to applicable Consortium codes of conduct and ethical guidelines.

### **Non-Lobbying Disclaimer**

The SIG does not engage in lobbying for specific legislation, regulatory outcomes, or commercial interests. Contributions are informational and educational only.

## **Success Metrics, KPIs, and Risk Indicators**

#### **Success**

The SIG will propose OKRs to the GB to be agreed and ratified once the work of the group has started.

## **Elevator Summary**

The Regulatory and Standards SIG is a neutral forum helping regulators and standards bodies understand how Confidential Computing supports protections for data in use while maintaining governance and oversight and to encourage adoption of appropriate measures.
