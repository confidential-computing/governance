## Announcements

Dan Middleton (DM) proposed to open up the TAC mailing for archives to be publicly available. No one objected to the proposal. 

## Project Update

Caroline Perez-Vargas provided an update on the OpenVMM project, explaining that while it is currently not fully open source due to its use in millions of production Azure VMs, Microsoft is committed to moving it to a neutral GitHub organization to meet CCC requirements. The team is working on a plan to handle this complex migration.

## TAC Operations

Simon Gallagher and Rene Kolga discussed guidance documents, with Simon planning to write drafts by the next meeting and aiming for a concise format with an executive summary and optional detailed sections. The group agreed to target guidance document drafts for review by the next meeting, with a goal to complete them in Q1.

Fritz Alder raised questions about the format of future TAC talks, seeking input on whether members prefer theoretical presentations, open source project discussions, or other technical topics.

### Technical Presentation Format Discussion

The group discussed the format and content of technical presentations at their meetings. Fritz proposed limiting the number of presentations to avoid overwhelming attendees, but Mike Bursell and others argued for maintaining variety, including academic presentations, open source discussions, technical introductions, and architectural reviews. The group agreed to continue with technical talks, with Fritz planning to establish clearer guidelines for timing and content. The group discussed tech talks and their value in attracting important decisions and increasing meeting attendance. Fritz was identified as the main point of contact for nominations, with guidance that talks should focus on community value rather than marketing. 

## NIST

The team also addressed a UK government call for information on confidential computing, with Mike Bursell noting the deadline and expressing concern about managing both that and the NIST response. Mike encouraged member companies to submit their own responses to the UK government request, and the group agreed to review the NIST document to determine if it could be repurposed for the UK submission. 

The team discussed and agreed to move the internal approval deadline to the 5th, allowing more time for preparation. They decided to attribute the document to the CCC as a whole rather than individual contributors, and Mike proposed removing extensive narrative sections while preserving relevant content. The group also agreed to focus on CC-specific information, with Mike planning to review and accept comments while specifying where confidential computing is relevant.

## NIST RFI

The rest of the meeting was dedicated to discussion and clarification of the response document. Good progress has been made, and the discussion would continue in the document, email and slack.
