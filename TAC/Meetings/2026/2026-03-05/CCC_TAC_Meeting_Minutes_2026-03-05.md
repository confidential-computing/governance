## Meeting Minute Approval & Old Business

* Previous TAC meeting minutes will be approved via GitHub; DM requested members review them before the next call.

## Announcements

* Updates were shared on TAC tech talks, the upcoming OC3 hybrid event, and a planned Confidential Computing Summit in June.

## NIST IR 8320 Update Planning

* Mike Bartock (MBa) outlined a plan to update NIST IR 8320 and requested up to two co-authors per interested company.
* MBa will clarify co-authorship details with NIST and coordinate access to the draft document.

## NIST RFI Response – Final Review and Vote

* Mike Bursell (MBu) led a final review of the NIST RFI response, focusing on references, CCC context, and minor editorial changes.
* Ofir will propose additional text on model protection and remote attestation; members were asked to add reference materials before the weekend.

### Vote: Approval of NIST RFI Response

* DM called for a vote to approve the NIST RFI response as the TAC’s position.
* Voting representatives from NVIDIA, Google, AMD, TikTok, and Microsoft approved; no objections or abstentions were recorded.
* **Result**: With quorum and unanimous approval from all voting representatives present, the NIST RFI response was approved as the TAC-endorsed document.
* MBu will finalize the document with agreed changes and submit it to NIST by March 9; TAC voting members will record their agreement via the designated GitHub issue or channel.

## Confidential Computing Blueprints

* Simon presented Blueprint B (secure key release); feedback covered terminology, removal of provider-specific diagrams, and additional examples. Simon will update the draft.
* Rene presented short and long versions of Blueprint C (clean room / collaborative computing); both will be maintained, with the short outline prioritized. Rene and Mingshen will circulate drafts and collect feedback.

## Levels of Integration Document

* Dan presented a draft “levels of integration” framework for confidential computing deployments.
* The TAC agreed to proceed with the framework; Dan will add a disclaimer that Level 1 (no attestation) is not recommended for security-sensitive use cases, and members will review risks/benefits and examples.

## Standards and External Coordination

* The TAC briefly discussed coordination with NIST and ISO/SC27 on confidential computing topics.
* Eric Hibbard (Samsung) and Alec (Microsoft) will coordinate on ISO/SC27 activities, with Alec to provide an update after the next meeting.

## Any Other Business / Next Steps

* Members were reminded to review the NIST documents, blueprints B and C, and the levels-of-integration draft, to suggest additional examples, and to confirm OC3 in-person attendance where applicable.
* Next meeting is scheduled on Thursday, March 19, 2026. DM adjourned the meeting at 8:59 am PT.
