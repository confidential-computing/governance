### TAC Tech Talk - Overview of TCG Confidential Computing

Henk Birkholtz (HB) introduced himself and the topic of Transparent Confidentiality, and provided a discussion on TCG. Discussion ensued over the definition of confidential computing and the role of a hardware-based TEE.

### Enarx - annual project review 

Nick Vidal (NV) and Mike Bursell (MB) provided an overview of Enarx and how it has evolved. NV noted that Enarx is a deployment framework for applications in TEEs. He noted that it provides a WASM runtime, and is designed to work across architectures transparently. It supports Intel's SGX and AMD's SEV. Currently Enarx is a Sandbox project.

NV noted there were changes to the project's communication channels. He noted that [https://blog.enarx.dev](https://blog.enarx.dev) is now an official channel, and that the project recently rebuilt [enarx.dev](https://enarx.dev).

NV noted some changes to third-party dependencies.

**Action:** BW to verify that third-party dependencies appear in the [CCC dependencies spreadsheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1UKnbbGWXYLjnPZsox3zmYo59nv3XSXjePfas5E2fER0/edit#gid=0).

MB noted that the Rust implementation of OpenSSL has been challenging for the project, and reviewed alternate implementations. He invited others to participate in the development process. DM noted he's aware of some other alternatives. MB reviewed the status of Rust-native implementations.

NV noted Enarx has begun to make formal releases and reviewed the release process. NV next reviewed the project's progression status. He reviewed the history of the project, and noted that there is continued collaboration with Red Hat. He noted that the project is working towards the requirements to diversify maintainers.

NV noted that the [project is using LFX Insights](https://insights.lfx.linuxfoundation.org/projects/ccc%2Fccc-enarx/dashboard;quicktime=time_filter_3Y) to measure progress and growth within the community. He reviewed community growth in the core team and outside contributors.

He noted that Enarx is not ready to request a move to Incubation stage, but that the project is showing a lot of continued growth. He noted the project does not yet feel it has met the requirements to have widespread adoption in at least two end-users.

NV reviewed the budget, noting that the project had requested a server from CCC. He noted that work is ongoing to stabilize infrastructure. MB discussed the use of the servers. NV noted that many community members have been granted access.

NV reviewed the project's participation in the Outreachy mentorship program. He noted it has had a major impact on the project. MB noted that the interns were very impressive. They began with high-level contributions and are now making low-level contributions. NV noted that the interns had presented at OC3, and demoed machine learning, zero-proof knowledge, and networking. He noted that this was an excellent way to present progress in the projects. One of the interns will present at Open Source Africa. MB noted that there have been massive benefits to the project from using Outreachy. He noted that it's helped the project, the community, and the individuals who are participating.

DT noted that the one request from the Governing Board to the TAC for 2022 is "spend more money." He noted that projects should think about ways to spend money for maximum impact for projects and the communities. He suggested CI/CD runners as an example. Discussion ensued.

Finally, NV reviewed the project's license scans. He noted no license issues. He reviewed some questions about the LFX Security scans. He also noted that the project is attempting to follow the best practices for security.

DM noted that there are many things a project should do for security, and noted that he's happy to see Enarx attempting to do this work. He asked for clarification on the Enarx workload premise. Is the idea that any bytecode should run, or that you're building bytecode directly for Enarx?

MB noted that the ideal situation is that applications are built as WASM executables. He noted there is also a debug path using a kvm-hosted version of the Enarx TEE. Discussion ensued over the ideal time to adapt code to use Enarx. Discussion ensued over WASI.
